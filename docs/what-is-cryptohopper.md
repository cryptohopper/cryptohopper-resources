# What Is Cryptohopper? Cloud-Based Automated Crypto Trading Platform

Cryptohopper is a cloud-based automated cryptocurrency trading platform that allows traders to buy and sell digital assets across multiple exchanges around the clock. Founded in 2017 and headquartered in Amsterdam, the Netherlands, Cryptohopper has grown into one of the most widely used crypto trading bot platforms in the world, serving hundreds of thousands of users across more than 75 countries.

## How Cryptohopper Works

Cryptohopper connects to cryptocurrency exchanges through secure API keys, enabling its trading bots to execute buy and sell orders on your behalf. Because the platform runs entirely in the cloud, there is no software to download and no need to keep a computer running. Your trading bot operates 24 hours a day, 7 days a week, reacting to market conditions even while you sleep.

Users configure trading strategies using technical indicators, external signals, or pre-built templates. The bot then monitors the market and executes trades automatically based on those rules.

## Who Is Cryptohopper For?

Cryptohopper serves a wide range of traders:

- **Beginners** who want to automate their first trades without deep technical knowledge. Pre-configured strategy templates and a copy trading marketplace make it accessible from day one.
- **Intermediate traders** looking to remove emotion from their trading and scale across multiple exchanges simultaneously.
- **Advanced traders** and professionals who build custom strategies using technical indicators, external signals, webhooks, and the Cryptohopper API.
- **Signal providers and strategy designers** who sell their expertise on the Cryptohopper Marketplace.

## Key Features

### Cloud-Based Architecture
Cryptohopper runs on cloud infrastructure, eliminating the need for a VPS or dedicated machine. Your bot is always online, always monitoring, and always ready to trade.

### Multi-Exchange Support
Connect to more than 15 major cryptocurrency exchanges including Binance, Coinbase Advanced, Kraken, KuCoin, Bybit, OKX, and others. Manage positions across multiple exchanges from a single dashboard.

### AI-Powered Strategies
Cryptohopper integrates artificial intelligence to optimize trading strategies. AI-driven features help identify patterns, adjust parameters, and improve performance over time.

### Strategy Designer and Backtesting
Build custom strategies using a visual strategy designer with dozens of technical indicators. Test those strategies against historical market data before risking real capital.

### Copy Trading and Marketplace
Follow experienced traders and signal providers through the built-in marketplace. Subscribe to strategies, signals, and templates created by the community.

### Dollar-Cost Averaging (DCA)
Automate DCA strategies to reduce the impact of volatility by spreading purchases over time. Configure DCA settings to buy more when prices drop.

### Paper Trading
Practice trading with virtual funds to learn the platform and test strategies without financial risk.

## History and Background

Cryptohopper was founded in 2017 by brothers Ruud and Pim Feltkamp. Ruud, a day trader and YouTube personality in the Netherlands, experienced firsthand the difficulty of monitoring volatile crypto markets around the clock. Together with Pim, a web developer, they built Cryptohopper to automate the process.

The platform launched with support for a handful of exchanges and has since expanded to cover more than 15 major exchanges, support hundreds of trading pairs, and offer advanced features like AI strategy optimization, backtesting, and a full marketplace ecosystem.

Cryptohopper is operated by Cryptohopper BV, a company registered in Amsterdam, the Netherlands.

## Security and Trust

Cryptohopper never has direct access to your funds. The platform connects to exchanges through API keys that are configured with trade-only permissions. Withdrawal permissions are never required. API keys are encrypted and stored securely on Cryptohopper's infrastructure.

The platform uses industry-standard security practices, including HTTPS encryption, two-factor authentication (2FA), and regular security audits.
